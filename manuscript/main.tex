\begin{abstract}
\noindent
UCYN-A is a nitrogen-fixing cyanobacterial endosymbiont that has evolved into an organelle-like structure (nitroplast) in the marine alga \textit{Braarudosphaera bigelowii}. Approximately 900 host-encoded proteins are imported into UCYN-A via a novel C-terminal transit peptide (uTP), unlike the N-terminal signals used by mitochondria and chloroplasts.
\end{abstract}
